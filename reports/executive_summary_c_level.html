Payment Processing Performance: Executive Summary

Executive Overview

Our payment processing system has experienced a concerning performance decline, with a systematic 7.6 percentage point degradation in June 2019 alone (75.7% → 68.1%). The system currently operates at 69.6% overall acceptance rate, resulting in $127M in declined volume across our analysis period. This recent decline represents both an immediate business risk requiring urgent attention and a substantial revenue recovery opportunity.

Business Impact:

  • Current declined volume: $127M (30.4% of total $418M processing volume)
  • Transaction volume recovery opportunity: $15-50M annual additional transaction volume potential through systematic improvements
  • Risk exposure: Potential infrastructure vulnerability identified through June 2019 performance degradation analysis

Current Performance Analysis

Critical Issues Identified

Infrastructure Stability Concerns:

  • June systematic decline: 7.6 percentage point degradation over 4 weeks (75.7% → 68.1%)
  • Geographic concentration: Performance issues concentrated in high-value markets
  • Hourly vulnerability patterns: Specific time-of-day processing bottlenecks identified

Geographic Decline Concentration:

  • US market: 18.0 percentage point decline during June (78% → 60%) - primary concern area
  • France: 10.0 percentage point decline during June (74% → 64%) - secondary concern
  • Stable markets: Canada and UAE showed improvement during same period, confirming issue concentration

Operational Inefficiencies:

  • Business hours advantage: US (+5.6%), Canada (+6.3%) during local business hours
  • Currency processing gaps: EUR (-21.2% exchange volatility) and CAD (-2.6% decline) showing correlation with acceptance rate degradation
  • After-hours processing: Systematic underperformance during non-business hours across key markets

Root Cause Assessment

Analysis indicates multiple contributing factors:

  • Processing infrastructure stress: Systematic decline patterns suggest capacity or system degradation issues
  • Currency volatility impact: Exchange rate fluctuations correlating with acceptance rate declines for EUR/CAD transactions
  • Time-dependent processing: Clear performance variations based on local business hours and geographic time zones

Strategic Action Plan

Immediate Actions (Week 1-4)

1. GlobePay Investigation Escalation

  • Formal demand for comprehensive technical investigation into June 2019 infrastructure decline
  • Request: System logs, processing capacity reports, infrastructure change history
  • Timeline: 1-week response requirement
  • Outcome: Either immediate resolution or confirmation to pursue alternative solutions

2. Business Hours Optimization Implementation

  • Deploy static scheduling rules for local business hours processing
  • Target markets: US, Canada (highest business hours advantage)
  • Expected impact: +7.6 percentage points acceptance rate improvement (addressing June decline pattern)
  • Timeline: 3-week implementation

Short-Term Initiatives (Month 1-3)

3. Multi-Processor Risk Mitigation

  • Implement Stripe as secondary processor for redundancy
  • Start with problem regions (US/France) using 10% traffic routing
  • Benefits: Eliminate single point of failure, gain decline reason visibility
  • Timeline: 6-week basic integration + 8-week monitoring

4. Evaluate Failed Payment Recovery Partnership

  • Evaluate partnerships with specialized recovery services (e.g., Flycode-type solutions)
  • Target: 5-10% recovery of declined transactions (high confidence), 10-15% recovery (optimistic case)
  • Timeline: 12-week pilot + implementation

Medium-Term Strategic Improvements (Month 3-6)

5. Comprehensive Processor Optimization

  • Evaluate and implement multiple processors (Stripe, Adyen, regional options)
  • Develop intelligent routing logic for optimal acceptance rates
  • Timeline: 24-week full implementation

6. Currency Processing Enhancement

  • Local currency charging for EUR/CAD markets to eliminate exchange rate volatility
  • Timeline: 16-week implementation + monitoring
