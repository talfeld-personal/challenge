Payment Processing Analysis - Key Assumptions

1. January Data Exclusion

Assumption: January represents the company's launch period and should be excluded from the main analysis.

Why:

  • Very small transaction amounts (likely test transactions or contractor payments)
  • All chargebacks occurred in January, suggesting early customer issues
  • Customers may not have been fully legitimate during launch phase
  • No chargebacks after January indicates stabilization

Impact: Analysis focuses on February-June operational data. Since the main decline is in June, removing January doesn't affect the core problem investigation.

2. Card-on-File Transaction Model

Assumption: Most transactions are card-on-file (saved payment methods) rather than manual card entry.

Why:

  • CVV data is missing for the vast majority of transactions
  • CVV is typically required for manual card entry
  • Absence suggests automated/programmatic payment processing

Impact: CVV analysis is excluded since it's not applicable to this transaction model.
